## Section 3 — Problem Statement

### What problem are you solving?

Nigeria's agricultural commodity sector — particularly in the Northwest zone, which produces the majority of the country's sesame, cowpea, groundnut, dried ginger, and castor seeds — operates almost entirely outside any digital infrastructure. Three interconnected and mutually reinforcing problems define this landscape:

---

**PROBLEM 1: INVISIBLE SUPPLY CHAIN**

Over 70% of commodity movements from farm to aggregator to exporter occur through informal traders with zero digital traceability. No harvest records, no origin documentation, no handling chain data. When international buyers — particularly in Europe, the GCC, and North Africa — request certificates of origin, phytosanitary clearance, or aflatoxin compliance documentation, most Nigerian exporters cannot produce them. This disqualifies Nigerian commodities from premium markets.

---

**PROBLEM 2: SMALLHOLDER FARMER EXPLOITATION**

Because farmers have no access to real-time market price information, they are structurally dependent on local middlemen who offer prices 30–50% below actual market rates. With no price transparency, no direct buyer access, and no digital identity, smallholder farmers — who constitute over 90% of Northwest Nigeria's agricultural workforce — have no leverage, no bargaining power, and no path to fair value.

---

**PROBLEM 3: EXPORT DOCUMENTATION BOTTLENECK**

The Nigerian Export Promotion Council (NEPC) data consistently shows that agri-export growth is constrained not by commodity availability but by documentation and compliance failures. Exporters spend weeks manually collecting farm records, lab results, and handling certificates that should take hours. Smaller operators simply exit the export market due to this friction. The result is a systemic underperformance of Nigeria's multi-trillion-naira commodity export potential.

---

### Who is most affected?

Three primary stakeholder groups are directly affected:

- **Smallholder farmers** (4.2M+ in the Katsina–Kano–Zamfara belt) — trapped in price exploitation cycles with no market data access.
- **Commodity aggregators and off-takers** (estimated 12,000+ active in Northwest Nigeria) — losing export contracts due to compliance and documentation failures.
- **International commodity buyers** (EU, GCC, Morocco, Southeast Asia) — unable to source certified, traceable Nigerian commodities despite strong demand.

The problem also affects government agencies (BOI, NEPC, CBN, State MinAgric) that lack reliable agricultural production and trade data for policy-making.

---

## Section 4 — Your Solution

### What are you building?

AgriTrace is a three-layer digital platform that digitises Nigeria's agricultural supply chain from farmgate to export:

---

**LAYER 1 — WHATSAPP FARMER INTERFACE**

Farmers register on AgriTrace via a WhatsApp chatbot — no smartphone app required, no data literacy barrier. Each harvest batch is logged through a conversational flow, generating a unique QR-coded digital certificate that travels with the commodity through every handler: transporter, aggregator, processor, and exporter. This creates an immutable, verifiable chain of custody for every commodity lot.

---

**LAYER 2 — AGGREGATOR SAAS DASHBOARD**

Commodity aggregators, processors, and exporters access a web dashboard to manage batch inventories, issue digital purchase receipts to farmers, track commodity movements, and — critically — auto-generate export compliance documents: phytosanitary certificates, certificates of origin, aflatoxin compliance logs, and batch manifests. A process that takes weeks manually is reduced to minutes.

---

**LAYER 3 — AI MARKET INTELLIGENCE ENGINE**

An AI engine aggregates real-time commodity prices from Lagos, Kano, Abuja, and Onitsha markets alongside Morocco, UAE, and EU import demand and price data. It pushes daily fair-price alerts to farmers via WhatsApp in Hausa and English, giving them market information parity with middlemen for the first time.

---

**MARKETPLACE LAYER (Phase 2)**

Verified domestic and international buyers browse fully traceable, certified commodity lots and connect with aggregators. Transactions are settled through an escrow mechanism powered by Paystack.

---

### How is your solution different?

AgriTrace's differentiation is fourfold:

1. **WhatsApp-first design** — no app download, works on basic smartphones, deployable to rural farmers immediately.
2. **Hausa language interface** — the first agricultural traceability tool built for Northern Nigeria's primary spoken language.
3. **Integrated export compliance automation** — eliminating the documentation bottleneck that blocks Nigerian SME exporters from international markets.
4. **Nigeria-Morocco trade corridor specialisation** — leveraging the founder's unique research and network in the Trans-Saharan agricultural trade route.
